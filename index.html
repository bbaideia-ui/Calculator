What Is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is interest earned on both your original investment and the interest that has already been added to your balance. This allows your money to grow faster over time.

How This Calculator Works

Enter your starting investment, monthly contribution, interest rate, number of years, and compounding frequency. The calculator estimates your future balance, total contributions, and total interest earned.

Compound Interest Formula

The basic compound interest formula is A = P(1 + r/n)nt. In this formula, A is the future value, P is the principal, r is the annual interest rate, n is the number of compounding periods per year, and t is the number of years.

Example

If you invest $1,000, add $100 per month, earn 7% annually, and invest for 10 years, this calculator estimates how much your money may grow.
