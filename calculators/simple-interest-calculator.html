What Is Simple Interest?

Simple interest is interest calculated only on the original principal amount. Unlike compound interest, it does not add previous interest back into the balance.

How to Use This Simple Interest Calculator

Enter the principal amount, annual interest rate, and time in years. The calculator will estimate total interest, final amount, and yearly balance growth.

Why Simple Interest Matters

Simple interest is commonly used for basic loans, short-term calculations, and educational examples because it is easy to understand and predictable.

Simple Interest Formula

The basic simple interest formula is I = P × r × t, where P is principal, r is the annual interest rate, and t is time in years.

Example Calculation

If you start with $1,000 at a 5% annual simple interest rate for 10 years, the total interest would be $500 and the final amount would be $1,500.
