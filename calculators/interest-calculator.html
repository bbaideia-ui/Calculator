What Is an Interest Calculator?

An interest calculator estimates how much money may grow over time based on an initial balance, contributions, interest rate, compounding frequency, taxes, and inflation.

How This Interest Calculator Works

Enter your starting amount, contribution amounts, interest rate, compounding frequency, investment length, tax rate, and inflation rate. The calculator estimates ending balance, total principal, contributions, interest earned, and inflation-adjusted value.

Compound Interest

Compound interest means interest can grow on both the original principal and previously earned interest. More frequent compounding may increase the ending balance over time.

Contributions

Regular contributions can significantly increase long-term growth. This calculator allows annual and monthly contributions and lets you choose whether contributions happen at the beginning or end of the period.

Tax and Inflation Adjustments

Taxes may reduce interest earned, while inflation may reduce purchasing power over time. This calculator includes optional tax and inflation inputs to create a more complete estimate.

Example Calculation

If you start with $20,000, contribute $5,000 per year, earn 5% interest, and invest for 5 years, this calculator can estimate your ending balance and total interest earned.
